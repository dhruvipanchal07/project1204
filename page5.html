Graphic Design

Duration: 3 Months to 5 Months


A typical graphic design syllabus covers fundamental design principles, typography, color theory, software proficiency, and various design specializations like branding, publication, and digital design. It also often includes the history of graphic design and visual communication techniques. 

       
 Design Principles:
     
  • Elements and principles of design (balance, contrast, hierarchy, etc.).
    •  

       
 Typography:
     
  •  Letterforms, typeface selection, spacing, alignment, and legibility. 
    •  

       
 Color Theory:
     
  •  Understanding color harmonies, psychology of color, and how color affects design. 
    •  

       
 Software Proficiency:
     
  •  Using industry-standard software like Adobe Photoshop, Illustrator, and InDesign. 
    •  

       
 Branding and Identity:
     
  •  Creating brand visuals, logos, and visual guidelines.
    •  
 
       
 Publication Design: 
     
  • Creating layouts for print materials like magazines and books. 
    •  

       
 Digital Design:
     
  •  Designing for websites, mobile apps, and other digital interfaces. 
    •  

       
 History of Graphic Design:
     
  •  Understanding the evolution of graphic design and its impact on society. 
    •  

       
 Visual Communication:
     
  •  Principles of effective visual communication and conveying messages through design. 
    •  

       
 Project-Based Learning:
     
  •  Developing portfolios through design projects. 
    •  


    
Example Syllabus Structure (simplified):


Semester 1:
     
  •  Design Fundamentals, Color Theory, Typography, Basic Software.
    •  


Semester 2:
     
  •  Branding and Identity, Publication Design, Digital Design Basics.
    •  


Semester 3:
     
  •  Advanced Software, Illustration, Motion Graphics (if applicable).
    •  


Semester 4:
     
  •  Specialization Projects, Portfolio Development, Internship Preparation.

2D/3D Animation

Duration: 5 Months to 7 Months

A typical 2D and 3D animation syllabus covers a range of topics, including drawing fundamentals, software proficiency, character design, and animation techniques. It also includes elements like storyboarding, sound design, and visual effects. 

2D Animation Syllabus:


Fundamentals: 
  •  sketching, drawing principles, and understanding of perspective.
    •  


Software:
 
  • Working with Adobe Illustrator, Photoshop, and other 2D animation software like Flash/Animate. 


Character Design:
  •  Creating characters with distinct visual characteristics and personalities. 


Animation Techniques:
 
  • Learning about motion graphics, keyframing, and timing. 


Digital Painting:
  •  Creating and editing digital artwork. 


Sound Design:
  •  Understanding the role of sound in animation and incorporating it effectively. 


Visual Effects:
  • Basic principles and techniques of visual effects in 2D animation.


3D Animation Syllabus:


3D Modeling: 
  • Learning to create 3D objects and characters using software like Maya or Cinema 4D.
 

Animation Techniques:
 
  • Mastering 3D animation techniques, including rigging, motion capture, and keyframing. 


Texturing and Lighting:
 
  • Understanding how to apply textures and lighting to 3D models. 


Camera Work:
 
  • Learning how to manipulate the camera within a 3D environment. 


Storyboarding and Scriptwriting:
  •  Developing a visual narrative and writing scripts for animation projects. 


Game Development Fundamentals:
  •  Understanding game mechanics and animation techniques for creating games.


Visual Effects:
 
  • Learning how to create and integrate visual effects into 3D animation. 


Sound Design:
 
  • Incorporating sound effects and music to enhance the animation experience.
